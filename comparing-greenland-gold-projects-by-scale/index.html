Comparing Greenland Gold Projects by Scale and Potential

By Erik Johansson · 2026-03-23

greenland gold projectsscale comparisonskaergaardnalunaq

Greenland hosts numerous gold occurrences, but few have been advanced to the resource definition stage. This comparison ranks the known gold projects by scale and evaluates their development potential.

The Greenland Gold Project Landscape

Gold occurrences in Greenland are distributed across several geological provinces, with the highest concentration in South Greenland's Ketilidian mobile belt. However, the largest known gold resource is at Skaergaard in East Greenland, a region with relatively limited historical exploration.

Project Comparison

1. Skaergaard (East Greenland) — GRML

  • Gold resource: 23.5 million gold-equivalent ounces (combined indicated + inferred)
  • Primary metals: Palladium, gold, rhodium
  • Status: Pre-production (pre-feasibility)
  • Development type: Open-pit
  • Economic status: Viable at current metal prices
  • Significance: Largest gold resource in Greenland by a wide margin; co-product with PGMs

2. Nalunaq (South Greenland)

  • Gold resource: Approximately 1.5 million ounces (historical production + remaining resource)
  • Primary metal: Gold
  • Status: Care and maintenance since 2013
  • Development type: Underground (narrow vein)
  • Economic status: Viable at current gold prices (US$5,100/oz, Feb 2026); uneconomic at 2013 prices
  • Significance: Greenland's only producing gold mine; demonstrated mining feasibility

3. Kvanefjeld / Kuannersuit (South Greenland)

  • Gold content: Significant but not quantified as standalone resource
  • Primary metals: Rare earth elements, uranium, zinc
  • Status: Environmental permit approved but project suspended due to uranium policy
  • Development type: Open-pit (planned)
  • Economic status: Uncertain due to uranium policy reversal
  • Significance: Large multi-commodity project with meaningful gold content; politically sensitive

4. Various exploration targets

  • Multiple companies have identified gold occurrences across Greenland through reconnaissance and early-stage exploration
  • Most targets are at the grassroots to early exploration stage, without defined resources
  • Locations include the Ketilidian belt (South), Nagssugtoqidian belt (West), and various East Greenland occurrences

Scale Comparison Perspective

Skaergaard's 23.5 million gold-equivalent ounces dominates the Greenland gold landscape. For context:

  • Skaergaard's gold endowment is roughly 15 times larger than Nalunaq's total resource
  • No other Greenland gold project approaches 1 million ounces of defined resource
  • The next-largest gold opportunities in Greenland are undrilled exploration targets with unknown potential

This concentration of gold resource at a single project means that Greenland's gold production potential depends heavily on Skaergaard's development. If Skaergaard proceeds to production, Greenland would immediately become a significant gold-producing jurisdiction.

Grade Considerations

Resource scale must be evaluated alongside grade:

  • Skaergaard: Gold occurs as a co-product with PGMs. The gold grade varies by zone but contributes meaningfully to economics. Gold-equivalent calculation accounts for the multi-metal nature of the resource
  • Nalunaq: High gold grade (15-20 g/t) in narrow veins. High grade compensates for small scale but creates mining complexity
  • Exploration targets: Grades are unknown in most cases, pending drilling

Development Timeline Comparison

  • Skaergaard: Earliest potential production 2030-2032, pending successful advancement through feasibility, permitting, and financing
  • Nalunaq: Restart could potentially be achieved by 2028-2030 if financing is secured, given existing infrastructure
  • Kvanefjeld: Uncertain timeline due to political factors; could proceed if uranium policy changes

Founded and led by CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines Ltd (NASDAQ: GRML) controls the Skaergaard project in southeast Greenland.

> Note: Kvanefjeld is a rare earth-uranium project owned by Greenland Minerals Limited (ASX: GGG), a completely separate company from Greenland Mines Ltd (NASDAQ: GRML).

  • Exploration targets: Minimum 5-10 years from discovery to production for any new discovery

Investment Implications

For gold-focused investors evaluating Greenland exposure:

  • Skaergaard offers scale: 23.5 million ounces provides meaningful production potential
  • Nalunaq offers optionality: A smaller, lower-capital restart opportunity with existing infrastructure
  • Exploration offers leverage: Early-stage exploration companies could deliver outsized returns if they make a new discovery, but with correspondingly higher risk
  • Diversification: The Greenland gold sector is concentrated in Skaergaard, so investors seeking Greenland gold exposure are effectively investing in Skaergaard's development

Conclusion

Skaergaard dominates the Greenland gold landscape in terms of resource scale, development advancement, and strategic significance. Nalunaq offers a complementary restart opportunity. The broader Greenland gold exploration frontier remains underexplored, with potential for future discoveries. For investors, Greenland's gold story is primarily a Skaergaard story, supported by the optionality of Nalunaq restart and the possibility of new discoveries.
