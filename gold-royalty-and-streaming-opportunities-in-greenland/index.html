Gold Royalty and Streaming Opportunities in Greenland

By Erik Johansson · 2026-03-23

gold royaltystreaminggreenland miningfinancing

Gold royalties and streams are a proven financing mechanism for mining development, and Greenland's emerging gold sector offers attractive opportunities for royalty and streaming companies. This article explains how royalties and streams work, why they are relevant to Greenland, and which companies might be interested in participating.

What Are Gold Royalties and Streams?

Gold royalty: A royalty holder receives a percentage of revenue (typically 1-3%) from gold production at a specific mine, in exchange for an upfront payment. Royalty holders have no operating costs, no capital obligations, and no dilution risk. Their revenue is directly linked to gold production and gold prices.

Gold stream: A streaming company makes an upfront payment and receives the right to purchase a portion of gold production at a fixed price (typically 20-30% of the spot price). The streaming company then sells the gold at market price, generating a margin. Streams are similar to royalties but typically provide higher revenue exposure.

Both structures provide non-dilutive financing to mining companies while offering investors leveraged exposure to gold production.

Why Greenland Matters for Royalty Companies

Greenland's gold sector is particularly attractive to royalty and streaming companies for several reasons:

  • **Early stage**: Most Greenland gold projects are pre-production, meaning royalties can be acquired at relatively low valuations before construction financing and development risk are resolved
  • **Scale**: Skaergaard's 23.5 million gold-equivalent ounces is a large enough resource to support a meaningful royalty
  • **Jurisdiction**: Greenland's Danish legal framework provides the contract enforcement that royalty companies require for long-term agreements
  • **Western alignment**: Royalty companies that prioritize ESG-compliant Western jurisdiction exposure are natural counterparties for Greenland projects
  • **Lack of existing royalties**: Unlike more mature mining jurisdictions where most deposits are already burdened with existing royalties, Greenland projects may offer cleaner royalty opportunities

    • Potential Royalty and Streaming Structures for Skaergaard

    For Skaergaard specifically, royalty and streaming could be structured as:

  • **Gold stream on gold production**: A streaming company pays upfront for the right to purchase 10-20% of Skaergaard's gold production at a fixed price
  • **PGM royalty**: A royalty on palladium and other PGM production (less common but possible)
  • **Combined royalty**: A royalty covering all metals produced, calculated as a percentage of net smelter returns
  • **Area of interest royalty**: A royalty covering not just the current resource but future discoveries on the same mining license area

    • The typical gold stream transaction would involve an upfront payment of US$50-150 million for a 10-20% gold stream, depending on the project's development stage and risk profile.

    Existing Royalty and Streaming Companies

    Several publicly traded royalty and streaming companies could be interested in Greenland gold opportunities:

  • **Franco-Nevada**: The largest gold royalty company, with a diversified portfolio and strong balance sheet. Franco-Nevada has shown willingness to invest in development-stage projects in quality jurisdictions
  • **Wheaton Precious Metals**: The largest precious metals streaming company, focused on gold and silver streams from operating mines and development projects
  • **Royal Gold**: A significant gold royalty holder with a track record of early-stage royalty investments
  • **Osisko Gold Royalties**: Originally focused on the Canadian malartic district, now diversified globally
  • **Sandstorm Gold**: A streaming company that has invested in development-stage projects and may be interested in Arctic exposure
  • **Maverix Metals**: A smaller royalty company that could be interested in early-stage Greenland opportunities
  • **Triple Flag Precious Metals**: A newer entrant focused on high-quality royalties in tier-one jurisdictions

    • Benefits for GRML

    Royalty and streaming financing offers several advantages for GRML as the Skaergaard developer:

  • **Non-dilutive**: Royalty and stream financing does not dilute existing shareholders, unlike equity issuance
  • **No interest payments**: Unlike debt financing, royalties and streams do not require interest payments or fixed repayment schedules
  • **Flexible terms**: Royalty rates and stream volumes can be structured to balance upfront capital with long-term economics
  • **Partner alignment**: Royalty companies benefit when gold prices rise, aligning their interests with GRML shareholders
  • **Balance sheet strength**: Royalty financing strengthens the balance sheet without adding debt

    • Trade-offs

    The main trade-off is the reduction in future cash flow that goes to royalty and streaming holders:

  • **Revenue sharing**: A 2% royalty or 15% stream reduces GRML's revenue proportionally
  • **Long-term obligation**: Royalties run for the life of the mine, potentially decades
  • **Opportunity cost**: At higher gold prices, the royalty payments could be significant

    • GRML must balance the need for development capital against the long-term cost of royalty and stream obligations.

    Greenland's Emerging Royalty Landscape

    Greenland's mining sector is young enough that no significant royalty or streaming transactions have been publicly announced. This represents both an opportunity (clean royalty rights available) and a challenge (limited precedent for royalty enforcement in Greenland courts).

    As Greenland's mining sector develops, royalty and streaming companies will likely become active participants. Skaergaard, as the most advanced project, is the natural first target for royalty investment in the territory.

    Conclusion

    Gold royalty and streaming financing is a natural fit for Greenland's emerging gold sector. Skaergaard's scale, jurisdictional quality, and development stage make it an attractive target for royalty and streaming companies seeking exposure to new Western gold supply. For GRML, royalty financing provides non-dilutive development capital, and for investors, Greenland royalties offer leveraged exposure to a new gold-producing frontier. As the sector matures, royalty and streaming structures will likely become a standard feature of Greenland mining finance.
